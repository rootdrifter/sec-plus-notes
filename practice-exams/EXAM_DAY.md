# Exam Day — SY0-701 protocol

The plan for the 24 hours around the exam. No new material at this stage — the gains now are from
calm, pacing, and not falling for traps you already know.

## The day before

- **One pass of `../LAST_MINUTE_GUIDE.md`** — the per-domain one-pager. Read it; don't re-study it.
- **One pass of `../MEMORY_AIDS.md`** — the mnemonics and the easily-confused pairs.
- **Do not** start a new practice exam or new topic. A bad score the night before damages confidence
  more than the review helps; cramming new material the night before is counterproductive.
- Confirm logistics: test centre address or OnVUE system check, ID ready, exam start time, route/time.
- Sleep. Rest beats one more hour of review — the exam tests recall under time pressure, and recall
  degrades with fatigue far faster than knowledge does.

## The morning of

- **Read `EXAM_TRAPS.md` once** — the specific gotchas (near-synonyms, "BEST/MOST/FIRST" framing,
  the order-of-operations questions). This is the highest-yield 10 minutes available to you.
- Eat. Hydrate (but mind the 90-minute no-break window). Arrive/log in **early** — the PBQs hit while
  you should be calm, not flustered from a rushed start.

## During the exam

- **PBQs (first, hardest):** read *every* option and the full scenario before touching anything. PBQs
  reward methodical reading and punish rushing. If one is sinking you past ~4 minutes, **flag it and
  move** — you can return; a lost easy MCQ is a worse trade.
- **MCQs:** **eliminate first, then choose.** Two options are usually obviously wrong; the question is
  almost always between the remaining two, and the qualifier (**BEST / MOST / FIRST / NEXT**) decides
  which. Answer what the qualifier actually asks, not the generically-correct-sounding option.
- **Flag and return.** Never burn time stuck. Eliminate, make your best choice, flag it, move on. Time
  is the scarce resource — an unanswered question scores zero, a guessed one has a chance.
- **Watch for "FIRST/NEXT" incident questions** — they test *order of operations* (e.g. contain
  before eradicate; identify before contain). The most thorough-sounding action is often not the
  *first* one.

## Where to spend any review time

By exam weight, a recovered mark is worth most here: **D4 Security Operations (28%)** → **D2 Threats
& Mitigations (22%)** → **D5 Program Management (20%)**. D5 is the one candidates underestimate —
governance, risk, third-party, and agreement types (MSA/SOW/SLA/MOU/BPA) carry a fifth of the exam.

## Immediately after

- If you passed: log it, then update the CV/portfolio (`PORTFOLIO_MAP.md` already maps every objective
  to a build — the cert now backs the portfolio that already demonstrated the skills).
- If not: pull the score report's per-domain breakdown into `error-log.md`, target the lowest domain
  with its `sprint-d{n}.md`, and re-sit. A near miss is a calibration result, not a verdict.
