# Sprint Exam — Domain 5: Security Program Management & Oversight (20%)

10 hard D5 items: governance, risk, third-party risk, compliance/privacy, audits, and metrics.
Often under-studied for its 20% weight. Single best answer; key below the divider.

**Q1.** SLE = £200,000 and ARO = 0.5. The ALE is:
A) £400,000  B) £100,000  C) £200,000  D) £50,000

**Q2.** A control costs £30,000/yr; it cuts ALE from £90,000 to £50,000. Is it justified?
A) No — too expensive  B) Yes — £40,000 avoided loss > £30,000 cost  C) Cannot tell  D) Only if AV rises

**Q3.** Under GDPR, a personal-data breach must be reported to the supervisory authority within:
A) 24 hours  B) 72 hours  C) 30 days  D) Immediately, publicly

**Q4.** Which is a *contractual* standard rather than a law?
A) GDPR  B) HIPAA  C) PCI-DSS  D) SOX

**Q5.** RPO answers which question?
A) How fast we recover the service  B) How much data we can afford to lose  C) How reliable the hardware is  D) The cost of an incident

**Q6.** A SOC 2 **Type II** report differs from Type I in that it:
A) Is cheaper  B) Tests control *effectiveness over a period*, not just design at a point in time  C) Covers privacy only  D) Is self-attested

**Q7.** The BEST first step when onboarding a critical SaaS vendor is:
A) Sign immediately  B) A third-party risk assessment (security questionnaire, SOC 2 review, right-to-audit + breach clauses)  C) Trust their marketing  D) Buy cyber-insurance only

**Q8.** Separation of duties primarily prevents:
A) Slow processes  B) A single individual completing a sensitive transaction end-to-end (fraud/abuse)  C) Hardware failure  D) Phishing

**Q9.** A data owner vs a data custodian — the *owner*:
A) Patches the servers  B) Is accountable for classification and acceptable use of the data  C) Runs backups  D) Encrypts the disks

**Q10.** Risk *treatment* options are best summarised as:
A) Encrypt, hash, sign, verify  B) Accept, Avoid, Transfer (share), Mitigate (reduce)  C) Detect, deter, deny  D) Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover

## Answer Key — Sprint D5

1. **B** — ALE = SLE × ARO = £200,000 × 0.5 = £100,000.
2. **B** — avoided loss (£40k) exceeds cost (£30k) → justified.
3. **B** — 72 hours to the supervisory authority.
4. **C** — PCI-DSS is contractual (card brands), not legislation.
5. **B** — RPO = tolerable data loss (drives backup frequency).
6. **B** — Type II = effectiveness over a window; Type I = design at a point in time.
7. **B** — assess before you sign; bake security + breach clauses into the contract.
8. **B** — SoD blocks one person owning a whole sensitive process.
9. **B** — the owner is accountable for classification/use; the custodian implements controls.
10. **B** — Accept / Avoid / Transfer / Mitigate (the four treatments). (D is NIST CSF functions — a distractor.)

**Score:** 9–10 mastery · 7–8 solid · ≤6 re-read D5 risk formulas + compliance + the applied drills.
