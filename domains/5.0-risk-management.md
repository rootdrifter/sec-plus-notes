# 5.0 ‚Äî Risk Management

## 1. Risk fundamentals

- **Risk = likelihood √ó impact** (of a threat exploiting a vulnerability against an asset).
- **Threat** (the actor/event) √ó **vulnerability** (the weakness) √ó **asset value** ‚Üí risk.
- **Risk responses (treatment):**
  - **Mitigate/reduce** ‚Äî apply controls.
  - **Transfer/share** ‚Äî insurance, outsourcing.
  - **Avoid** ‚Äî stop the risky activity.
  - **Accept** ‚Äî formally tolerate (within appetite), with sign-off.
- **Residual risk** = risk left after controls; **inherent risk** = before controls; **risk
  appetite/tolerance** = how much the org will accept.

## 2. Risk assessment methods

- **Qualitative:** likelihood/impact rated (Low/Med/High), **risk matrix/heat map** ‚Äî fast, subjective.
- **Quantitative (know the formulas):**
  - **SLE** (Single Loss Expectancy) = **Asset Value √ó EF** (Exposure Factor).
  - **ARO** = Annualised Rate of Occurrence (times/year).
  - **ALE** (Annualised Loss Expectancy) = **SLE √ó ARO**.
  - Justify a control if its annual cost < the **reduction in ALE** it delivers.
- **Risk register:** living record of risks, owners, scores, treatments, status.
- **Assessment types:** one-time / continuous / ad-hoc; **control assessment**, **risk-based**.

> for finding severity (e.g. directory listing = medium-high), with CVSS as a first-order estimate ‚Äî
> the qualitative/quantitative distinction in practice.

## 4. Business continuity & disaster recovery

- **BIA (Business Impact Analysis):** identifies critical functions and the metrics below.
- **Key metrics (know them precisely):**
  - **RTO** (Recovery Time Objective) ‚Äî max tolerable *downtime*.
  - **RPO** (Recovery Point Objective) ‚Äî max tolerable *data loss* (time since last good backup).
  - **MTD** (Maximum Tolerable Downtime) ‚Äî RTO must be ‚â§ MTD.
  - **MTBF** (Mean Time Between Failures ‚Äî reliability), **MTTR** (Mean Time To Repair/Recover),
    **MTTF** (mean time to failure, non-repairable).
- **DR sites:** **hot** (ready, near-instant, costly), **warm** (partial, hours), **cold** (space
  only, days, cheap).
- **Plans:** BCP, DRP, **IRP** (incident response), **COOP**, communications plan, succession.
- **Testing:** **tabletop** (discussion) ‚Üí **walkthrough** ‚Üí **simulation** ‚Üí **parallel** ‚Üí **full
  interruption** (most realistic, most disruptive).
- **Order of restoration** and **redundancy** (see [domain 3](3.0-architecture-design.md) ¬ß5).

## 5. Incident response

- **Lifecycle (NIST SP 800-61):** **Preparation ‚Üí Identification/Detection ‚Üí Containment ‚Üí
  Eradication ‚Üí Recovery ‚Üí Lessons Learned**. (Some texts use PICERL.)
- **Containment:** short-term (isolate) vs long-term; **isolation, segmentation, sandboxing**.
- **Roles:** CSIRT/CIRT, incident commander, comms lead; **playbooks/runbooks**.
- **Forensics:** **order of volatility** (RAM/cache ‚Üí disk ‚Üí archives), **chain of custody**,
  **write blockers**, imaging, hashing for integrity, legal hold, e-discovery.

> logs; its [methodology-context](../../spectre/research/methodology-context.md) compares this to
> the **ACPO** forensic principles and the SOC IR lifecycle.

## 6. Data privacy, governance & compliance

- **Regulations:** **GDPR** (EU/UK ‚Äî personal data, breach notification, DPO, data-subject rights),
  HIPAA (US health), PCI DSS (cards), SOX, CCPA. Know GDPR is the big one for UK/EU markets.
- **Data roles:** **data owner**, **data controller** (decides purpose/means), **data processor**
  (acts on controller's behalf), **data custodian/steward** (day-to-day handling), **DPO**.
- **Data lifecycle:** classification ‚Üí retention ‚Üí **secure disposal/sanitisation** (wiping,
  degaussing, shredding, crypto-erase).
- **Agreements:** **SLA** (service levels), **MOU/MOA**, **BPA**, **NDA**, **ISA** (interconnection),
  **DPA** (data processing). Vendor/**supply-chain risk management** and third-party assessments.
- **Policies:** AUP, separation of duties, least privilege, onboarding/offboarding, clean desk,
  **security awareness training** (the human control ‚Äî ties to [mirage](../../mirage)'s phishing focus).

## 7. Security awareness & the human element

User training, **phishing simulations**, role-based training, gamification, recognising social
engineering, reporting culture. People are the most-attacked layer ‚Äî training is a **preventive +
directive** control.

> Portfolio link: [mirage](../../mirage) studies *why* social engineering works (urgency, authority,
> trust) ‚Äî the evidence base behind effective awareness programmes.

## 8. Audits, assessments & third-party risk

- **Audit vs assessment:** an **audit** checks conformance against a defined standard/control set
  (pass/fail, often by an independent/external party); an **assessment** evaluates posture and risk
  more broadly (findings + recommendations). **Internal vs external/independent** changes assurance
  weight.
- **Attestation & evidence:** **SOC 2 Type I** (controls *designed* at a point in time) vs **Type II**
  (controls *operating effectively* over a period); right-to-audit clauses in contracts.
- **Security testing types:** vulnerability assessment, **penetration test** (black/grey/white box ‚Äî
  domain 1 ¬ß5), **red/blue/purple team**, **bug bounty**, tabletop exercises.
- **Third-party / supply-chain risk:** vendor due diligence/questionnaires, SLAs and the agreements in
  ¬ß6, continuous vendor monitoring, **fourth-party** (your vendor's vendors), EOL/EOSL dependency
  risk, software provenance (SBOM ‚Äî domain 3 ¬ß8).

> Portfolio link: [spectre](../../spectre) is a **grey-box assessment with exploitation** ‚Äî it both
> *assesses* posture and *audits* the host against CIS/ISO baselines, and its scoping/rules-of-
> engagement and SHA-256 evidence chain are exactly the rigour an external attestation expects.

## 9. Security metrics, KPIs & reporting

- **Operational metrics:** **MTTD** (mean time to detect), **MTTR** (mean time to respond/recover),
  dwell time, alert volume / false-positive rate, patch latency, % assets compliant with baseline.
- **Risk indicators:** **KRI** (key risk indicator ‚Äî leading signal a risk is rising) vs **KPI**
  (performance against a target); track residual risk and control coverage.
- **Reporting up:** translate technical findings into business risk for the risk register and board ‚Äî
  the communication skill consultancies and SOCs screen for (interview point).
